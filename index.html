The Musician

Madmen 7:13

I have this image of you:


Quiet and reposed,
Over the thick nickel river crossings of Saskatchewan,
Ears dipped in sulfur run-off from the salt mines on high,
Your form refracted in milky saffron.

“Tastes real rusty,” Charon says, anchored by your silence,
“Well I am here, play for me. I can pay.
“Maybe another day,” you politely nod, outside of the sunlight

And off he swivels away, into the horizon’s silver.
I shiver along winter’s hips, walking lost in the ivory city,
Until the trails led me to your door, and the couch was warm.
“I share my songs with no one,” you told me, eyes trained
Above the rabble. And it took nearly a year before you cracked,
And in the last breath of sunset, you voice chanted for me
A melody in search of syllables.

“I can rhyme,” I think I stammered, But you smiled. “Let’s give it a try.” And everything un-musical was erased.

What was perfect on the page would embarrass on the stage.
As Caicai-Vilu and Trentren-Vilu failed to rebuild the world
After the waste of terrain and language.



End of Part I
